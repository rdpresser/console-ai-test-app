HEALTHCARE CLAIM AUDIT REPORT
Audit Period: Q1 2026
Prepared by: Claims Audit Department
Document Reference: AUD-2026-Q1-007

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report covers the audit of 847 healthcare claims processed during Q1 2026 across three provider networks. The total value under review amounts to $4.2 million. Key findings indicate a 12% denial rate, with the primary driver being missing pre-authorization documentation (38% of denials), followed by duplicate claim submissions (24%) and incorrect billing codes (21%).

SECTION 1 – CLAIM VOLUME AND FINANCIAL SUMMARY

Total claims received: 847
Total claims approved: 695 ($3.1M)
Total claims denied: 101 ($820K)
Total claims pending: 51 ($280K)

Top providers by claim volume:
1. City General Hospital – 312 claims – $1.4M
2. Regional Orthopedic Center – 198 claims – $980K
3. Metro Emergency Room – 156 claims – $620K
4. Riverside Family Clinic – 89 claims – $340K
5. Lakeside Diagnostics – 92 claims – $480K (highest denial rate: 28%)

SECTION 2 – DENIAL ANALYSIS

The most common denial reasons identified during this audit period are:

Missing pre-authorization (38% of denials): Claims submitted for procedures requiring prior approval from the payer were not accompanied by the corresponding authorization number. Staff training has been recommended for the top three offending providers.

Duplicate submissions (24% of denials): Multiple billing submissions for the same service date and patient were detected. This is a known issue with the legacy billing system at City General Hospital, which is scheduled for migration in Q2 2026.

Invalid or incorrect procedure codes (21% of denials): ICD-10 and CPT codes did not match the documented diagnosis or were outdated codes no longer accepted under the 2026 fee schedule.

Exceeded benefit limits (17% of denials): Claims exceeded the annual maximum benefit for specific categories including physical therapy (30 visits/year) and mental health services (60 days/year).

SECTION 3 – HIGH-RISK FLAGS

The following claims have been flagged for secondary review due to anomalies detected during automated screening:

CLM-2026-0421: $48,000 surgical claim submitted 90 days after service date. Provider: City General. Reason for flag: late submission combined with unusually high amount for outpatient procedure.

CLM-2026-0589: Duplicate claim for same patient (DOB: 1978-03-14) and procedure (knee arthroscopy) submitted by two different providers on the same date. Possible coordination-of-benefits issue.

CLM-2026-0712: Emergency room claim for patient admitted to a non-network facility. Pre-approval was not obtained. Patient eligibility for out-of-network benefits must be verified.

SECTION 4 – RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Implement automated pre-authorization validation at claim intake to reduce denial rate by an estimated 6-8%.
2. Upgrade the legacy billing system at City General Hospital to eliminate duplicate submissions.
3. Conduct provider education sessions on 2026 CPT/ICD-10 code updates before Q2 billing cycle.
4. Review benefit limit tracking for high-utilization patients to proactively notify members before limits are reached.

SECTION 5 – NEXT STEPS

A follow-up audit is scheduled for Q2 2026 (July 15). Providers with denial rates exceeding 20% will be subject to a corrective action plan. The claims processing team will implement the pre-authorization validation module by May 31, 2026.

END OF REPORT
